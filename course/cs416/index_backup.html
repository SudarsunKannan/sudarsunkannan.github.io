Overview

  • This course is designed for graduate students who understand OS fundamentals and have taken an introductory OS course
  • We will discuss the following topics (tentative): OS structure, virtual memory management, file system design, threads and scheduling, virtualization, and distributed systems (with focus on file systems).
  • For each topic, we will discuss historic papers and ideas, current influential papers, and future research trends.
  • Students must read the paper and submit a three-paragraph (basic ideas, pros, and scope for improvement) review of the paper before the class.
  • Deep insights on OS and related ideas requires extensive coding, debugging, and experimentation. This course is a project-intensive course. We will have three or four small homework coding projects and a semester-long research project. The homework projects must be solved individually. 
  • For semester-long group projects, you are encouraged to work in groups. The goal is to work on a research idea and write a short workshop paper. While you are more than welcome to propose your ideas, I will also help with topics.
  • There will be two exams - midterm and finals. The exams will be based on lectures, papers, and homework projects covered in the class.

Grading

  • Midterm - 10% 
  • Finals - 15% 
  • Homeworks - 30% 
  • Semester Projects - 35% 
  • Paper Reviews - 10%
